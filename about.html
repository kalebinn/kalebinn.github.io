About Kelvin Ma

Kelvin Ma is an undergraduate electrical engineering student at The City College of New York. During his time as an aspiring engineer, he has done extensive research in the field of artificial intelligence and game theory with Professor Uyar. The project was made possible by a grant provided byThe US Army Combat Capabilities Development Command. The grant was provided through the Research Foundation of the City University of New York. As a researcher, Kelvin has three publications to date.

In addition to being a researcher with RFCUNY, Kelvin Ma also conducts research with Professor Saeed in the field of Quantum Computing at The City College of New York . Using the Qiskit Framework and IBM Q-experience for quantum computing, he investigates the effects of quantum noise on machine learning, and graphing algorithms such as the support vector machine algorithm and the maxcut problem.

Kelvin Ma is also the current Vice-President of the IEEE City College of New York Student Chapter where he develops and leads technical workshops, professional development events, and is currently developing a program to aid in the transfer process of prospective engineering students from other schools in City University of New York. He also is the student representative for the Electrical Engineering Department during all faculty meetings within the Grove School of Engineering.

Most recently, Kelvin was made the Electrical and Computer Engineering Section Chief of the Harlem Launch Alliance , a non-profit organization that promotes and competes in various collegiate rocketry programs/competitions.
